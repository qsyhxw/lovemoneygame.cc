LoveMoney All Endings Guide

LoveMoney features 14 distinct endings based on your choices and interactions with Harvey. This guide covers all ending categories: Pure Heart (good), Compromise (neutral), and Point of No Return (bad), plus how to unlock the true ending.

True Ending Guide View All Endings

✅ Pure Heart Endings

Good endings achieved by reaching $25,000 while maintaining respect for Harvey's boundaries. Avoid purchasing intimate items.

⚠️ Compromise Endings

Neutral endings where you push boundaries but stop short of severe consequences. Results in awkward but not disastrous outcomes.

❌ Point of No Return

Bad endings from prioritizing money over morals. Crossing clear boundaries leads to severe consequences for Harvey.

The True Ending: "The Savior"

The Savior is considered the best good ending and the "true ending" of LoveMoney. This ending represents the most morally sound path where you successfully raise the $25,000 for your operation while maintaining a respectful relationship with Harvey.

How to Unlock The Savior

  • ✅ Reach the $25,000 goal through clicking and upgrades
  • ✅ Purchase basic items: feather, brush, wreath (optional)
  • ❌ DO NOT purchase lipstick
  • ❌ DO NOT purchase soap (final item)
  • ✅ Maintain Harvey's dignity and personal boundaries

In this ending, Harvey congratulates you on reaching your goal, and you get your operation without causing him harm or distress. This path emphasizes restraint and moral integrity.

All 14 Endings Overview

LoveMoney's 14 endings are determined by your purchasing decisions and interaction choices. While not all ending names are publicly documented, the confirmed endings include:

Pure Heart Endings (Good)

The Savior (Best Ending)

The true ending. Reach $25,000 without crossing Harvey's boundaries. Avoid purchasing lipstick and soap. Harvey remains comfortable and congratulates you on your success.

Compromise Endings (Neutral)

The Awkward Acquaintance

Reach $25,000 but Harvey is visibly flustered due to your forwardness. Purchase items like feather, brush, and wreath, engage in intimate actions, but avoid lipstick and soap. The relationship remains uncertain and uncomfortable.

Point of No Return Endings (Bad)

The Unwanted Advance

Purchase feather, brush, wreath, and lipstick. Choose aggressive and intimate actions. Harvey becomes deeply embarrassed and worried about his wife finding out, transforming the interaction into a purely transactional one.

The Ultimate Betrayal (Worst Ending)

The worst ending. Purchase every single item in the shop, including the final item: soap. This deeply distresses Harvey, making him fear he has cheated on his wife Eun-mi. The ending implies severe future repercussions.

Ending Strategy Guide

Your ending is determined by which items you purchase from the shop. Items unlock as you earn more money, and each purchase affects Harvey's comfort level and your relationship.

Key Decision Points

  • Feather, Brush, Wreath: Safe items that don't cross major boundaries
  • Lipstick: First major boundary - purchasing this leads to bad endings
  • Soap: Final item - triggers the worst ending if purchased

FAQ

How many endings does LoveMoney have?

LoveMoney has 14 distinct endings categorized into Pure Heart (good), Compromise (neutral), and Point of No Return (bad) paths.

What is the true ending?

"The Savior" is the true ending and best good ending. Reach $25,000 without purchasing lipstick or soap to unlock it.

What triggers the worst ending?

"The Ultimate Betrayal" is triggered by purchasing every item in the shop, including the final item (soap). This causes Harvey severe distress.

Can I get a good ending if I purchase some items?

Yes. You can purchase basic items like feather, brush, and wreath and still get good endings. The key is avoiding lipstick and soap.

Is LoveMoney related to BLOODMONEY?

Yes. LoveMoney is inspired by BLOODMONEY! and features the same character, Harvey. See Harvey Harvington Wiki for more context.
