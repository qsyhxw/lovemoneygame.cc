Understanding the Ending System

LoveMoney features a branching narrative system where your choices throughout the game determine which ending you receive. The most critical decision point identified by players is whether or not to purchase the final shop item known as the Soap. This single choice fundamentally changes Harvey's story trajectory and your relationship outcome.

Beyond this primary branch, your overall playstyle affects which specific ending variation you encounter. Factors include how quickly you earn money, which intermediate shop items you purchase, how you interact with Harvey's dialogue, and whether you explore optional content in the UPDATE version.

The game's core design creates genuine moral tension: faster routes to the $25,000 goal often require purchasing items that make Harvey increasingly uncomfortable, while slower ethical approaches preserve his dignity but extend gameplay time significantly.

üéØ Core Game Objective

Your goal is to earn $25,000 for a life-saving medical operation by clicking Harvey and purchasing shop upgrades. The ending scene triggers once you reach this amount, reflecting the path you took to get there.

The Critical Decision: Understanding the Soap

Multiple independent sources confirm that the Soap item serves as the primary branching point in LoveMoney's narrative structure. This item appears late in the shop progression and represents a clear moral threshold that fundamentally alters Harvey's story.

When you purchase the Soap, the game transitions into a second phase with different mechanics and significantly more intimate content. Harvey's dialogue shifts dramatically, expressing concern about his wife and the boundaries being crossed. Players report this decision cannot be reversed, making it the single most impactful choice in the game.

The Soap represents the game's central question: how far are you willing to go for money? Choosing not to purchase it demonstrates restraint and respect for Harvey's personal life, while purchasing it prioritizes efficiency and curiosity over ethical boundaries.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many endings does LoveMoney actually have? ‚ñº

The exact number is not officially confirmed by developer Buwu. Multiple sources claim "14 endings," but these claims cannot be independently verified through official channels or detailed player reports. Based on cross-referenced data, there appear to be 2-4 main ending paths (Friendship, Neutral, Bad, Secret) with possible variations within each path. The "14 endings" figure may count minor variations or dialogue differences as separate endings.

Can I get multiple endings in one playthrough? ‚ñº

No. The Soap purchase decision locks you into either the Friendship/Neutral path or the Bad path. You cannot see both in a single playthrough. To explore different endings, you must replay the game (30-60 minutes per run depending on playstyle) and make different choices. The UPDATE version does not appear to have a built-in save system according to developer comments, so most players complete runs in single sessions.

What's the difference between OLD and UPDATE versions? ‚ñº

The UPDATE version (released late 2024) contains: additional mechanics, secret content including the 12 notes and password screen, a new/expanded story, and various bug fixes. The OLD version has bugs, unfinished features, and translation issues. The official itch.io page offers both versions as separate downloads. All information in this guide applies to the UPDATE version unless specified otherwise.

Is Harvey's wife ever seen or named in the game? ‚ñº

Developer Buwu confirmed in itch.io comments that Harvey is "very devoted to his wife" and has a family, making this canon. One source mentions her name as "Eun-mi," though this has not been verified across multiple sources. Her presence is primarily referenced through Harvey's dialogue expressing concern about cheating on her. In bad endings, the game reportedly implies or shows consequences involving her discovery of what happened.

Why do some guides mention items like "Knife" and "Organs"? ‚ñº

These items belong to BloodMoney, the original game that inspired LoveMoney. BloodMoney features violent tools (Feather, Needle, Hammer, Scissors, Match, Knife, Gun) where you hurt Harvey to earn money. LoveMoney is a fan-made spin-off with a different tone‚Äîit focuses on intimacy and moral boundaries rather than violence. Some unreliable guides mistakenly mix content from both games. In LoveMoney, the Feather serves a playful/tickling purpose rather than a violent one.
